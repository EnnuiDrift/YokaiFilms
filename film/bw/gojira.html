  • Gojira

In the film, Gojira is defeated with the creation of the “Oxygen Destroyer” by Dr Serizawa. Thus, the use of the laws of chemistry are used to create this destructive and dangerous device. Science is also used to help explain the existence of Gojira. The palaeontologists and zoologists create an investigative team to explore the creation of Gojira through the activities of nuclear arms disturbing and contaminating it into a mutated creature. However, they also explained that it was a dinosaur that lived for so long. There, Gojira is a sort of combination. It is a creature that already existed deep in the ocean. Gojira later transformed into a kaiju as a result of human activity. Thus, humans, in particular, the scientists that created nuclear arms, helped to create the monster.

By Toho Company Ltd. (東宝株式会社, Tōhō Kabushiki-kaisha) © 1954 [Public domain], via Wikimedia Commons

As a result, Gojira, through the mutated kaiju, is used to reflect the belief in anti-nuclear arms. The destruction caused by the kaiju reflects the destruction created by the weapons of mass destruction dropped on Japan. This can be seen in the complete demolition of infrastructure. As well, there was also the scene of the families that suffered from dead loved ones and those separated which must have reflected the time after the atomic bombs were dropped on Japan. Thus, there is the belief in the disuse of these destructive technologies. The behaviour of Dr Serizawa reinforces this belief. In the film, he explicitly warns against the use of technology like the Oxygen destroyer as it would be used to devastating consequences. Thus, he sacrificed his life and work to destroy all traces of the technology when he used it to kill Gojira. Furthermore, there is also the direct warning of another kaiju monster like this appearing again with the counting practice of nuclear weapons. Hence, the film is set around the belief of stopping the use of nuclear arms and harm to nature which is also a central theme in Shinto.
