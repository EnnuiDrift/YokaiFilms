Ugetsu

The values and beliefs reflected in the Kuroneko coincide with a plethora of religious systems. There are some aspects of Buddhism and Shintoism apparent in these stories. There are ideas and concepts of Shinto in the film as seen in the use of the divine purification ceremony for Gintoki to purify him after coming into contact with a yokai. There are also elements of Daoism in reference to the sword alter. It is described as being embedded with the balance of yin and yang for it to offer divine protection. The stories also reflect the Japanese beliefs of monsters and their tie to animals. In the film for instance, there is an example of a bakeneko.
