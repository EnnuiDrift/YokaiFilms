Tanukis in Pom Poko

In the film, one of the master tanukis branched off and created a “Buddhist dance cult”. The tanuki failed in terrifying away the humans with their plot. Thus, in order to deal with their continued suffering, they created a Buddhist group to try to come to terms and deal with that suffering. This can be seen in their final act where the members of the cult climb on to the boat to some kind of afterlife.

A drawing of a tanuki from a traditional Japanese painting.

Public Domain Mark 1.0

As well, there is another instance where the Buddha is seen coming down to take the soul of the master tanuki that died from over-exerting himself. This reflects the Buddhist concept of the afterlife. Thus, the scene shows a visual representation of the Buddha taking the soul of the tanuki to continue living in another life.
