Modern Films:

Kwaidan

Kitaro

The values and beliefs reflected in the film both involve elements and the Onmyodo belief system. These elements can be seen in the practices and ceremonies of diviners in handling supernatural events. For instance, immediately in the beginning of the film, these influences can be seen in the ceremony to contain Prince Sawara’s angry spirit. The ceremony called on the spirits of the four directions.

In Onmyoji, the idea of Yin and Yang can be seen in the characters of Lady Aone and Prince Sawara. The Yang is represented by Prince Sawara who is a fiery and angry character which represents the active nature of Yang. On the other hand, Yin is represented by Lady Aone who is a more quieter force and she is often in the dark which represents the dark and passiveness of Yin. As seen in the film, when the two characters are joined, things become peaceful again as Lady Aone convinces Prince Sawara to cease his destructive path. This reflects the balance on the Yin and Yang ideology.

There is also the use of Shikigami (or genies in English) which are one of an onmyoji’s powers to control or create magical creatures. This can be seen in the paper servants that Seimei uses in the film.
