Kwaidan

In the Kitaro film are elements and concepts from Shintoism and the belief of curses. Although there are no direct mentions of religious beliefs of religious figures, there are concepts and practices that reflect religious systems. For instance, there are elements of Shintoism in the film as seen in the use of shrines for kami such as the fox spirits. There is also the idea of giving offerings to these kami. As seen in the film, the Tengu mentioned how she would like Kitaro to persuade humans to leave aburaage (fried tofu) offers for them. This corresponds to the Shinto practice of leaving offerings for kami to deal with them and offer respect to them.

The beliefs in curses is also evident in the film and story. As seen in the film, the power of thousands of grudges from yokai is stored in the jewel that the fox spirits are after. The power of the jewel is strong enough to bewitch humans. This could be seen as a type of curse as it is a material embodiment of emotions from many yokai.
