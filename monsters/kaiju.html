One of the most popular kaiju is Godzilla or pronounced Gojira in Japanese. A giant radioactive lizard coming from the depths and wreaking havoc and destruction on a city is the typical mode of operations for a typical kaiju monster. However, there are a great many other kaiju that have since appear since Godzilla such as King Kong the giant gorilla, giant insects, giant octopuses and so on.1

A scene from Godzilla Raids Again (1955) Godzilla vs Anguirus.

[Public domain], via Wikimedia Commons

The kaiju genre of films came about in 1953 when the first Godzilla movie was made. The film, titled "The Beast from 20,000 fathoms", was one of the first movies to 'associate nuclear weapons with giant, destructive creatures".2 The scene has since become filled with epic battle royales, comical at times. The genre of kaiju monsters have since expanded to not only include the destruction of cities, but also the major clashes against other kaiju in the back drop of Tokyo getting demolished. However, there is a deeper take on the genre if you take a closer look. The destruction by huge monsters, usually as a result of nuclear by-products, can reflect the fears and worries in industrialized city with the fast paced and sometimes destructive tendencies from scientific advancements such as world wars and the cold war.
