Buddhism is a large religion ranging all over the world. There are many variations and differences from country to country, region to region, and even within countries. In japan, there are many versions of Buddhism and these can be seen in many films. This page will go into a brief introduction to some of these sects and types of Buddhist practices in Japan. One of the main points of Buddhism in japan, is that Buddhism is associated with death. The Shinto religion relieves heavily on purity and death is considered a polluting practice, hence, Buddhist commonly take over and oversee funeral practices.1 this association is a common theme in films.

Yoshitoshi [Public domain], via Wikimedia Commons

As well, there are many other types of Buddhism in Japan that although may not be practiced currently, draw many inspirations in films. One of these being the sohei who are warrior monks. These were a sect of monks that took up arms, which is a drastic opposite from the peaceful image of typical Buddhists. the existence of these monks came about when Buddhism was brought to Japan and the differing sects often got into disputes with one another or even the imperial courts in Japan.2 As an imported religion to Japan where there was a lack of central authority to regulate Buddhism; this brought on the role of the sohei class which were often "deployed [sic] to intimidate potential rival schools and teachers".3 these warrior monks are a trope often seen in movies. Perhaps not sohei specifically, but the ideas of monks able to fight. this might have been the inspiration for Star Wars even!
